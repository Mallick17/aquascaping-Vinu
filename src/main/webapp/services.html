Frequently Asked Questions

Basic need to be known and Most asked questions.

What is aquascaping?

Aquascaping is the art of designing and arranging aquatic plants, rocks, and other natural elements in an aquarium to create a beautiful and harmonious underwater landscape.

What do I need to start aquascaping?

You'll need an aquarium, a substrate (such as gravel or soil), plants, hardscape materials (rocks, driftwood), water, a filtration system, lighting, and CO2 supplementation if you plan on growing high-maintenance plants.

How do I plan an aquascape design?

Start by considering the style you want (e.g., Iwagumi, Nature, Dutch). Use elements like the rule of thirds, the golden ratio, and focal points. Think about plant height, leaf color, and the use of hardscape to create depth and contrast.

Do I need to use CO2 for my aquascape?

CO2 is essential for growing many types of aquatic plants, especially in high-light, nutrient-rich setups. Low-tech aquascapes with slower-growing plants may not require CO2 supplementation.

How do I maintain the aquascape?

Regular maintenance includes pruning plants, cleaning algae, checking water parameters (pH, hardness, ammonia, nitrites, and nitrates), replacing water, and maintaining filtration systems.

What size aquarium should I choose?

The size depends on the space available and the types of fish or plants you want to keep. Beginners often start with a 20-40 gallon tank, which offers a manageable volume and more stability for water conditions.

What filtration system should I use?

The filtration system should be suitable for the tank size and type of aquarium. For planted tanks, a canister filter is often preferred as it provides powerful filtration without disturbing plant growth. For fish-only setups, hang-on-back (HOB) filters or sponge filters can work well.

What substrate should I use?

The substrate depends on the type of plants you're keeping. For planted aquariums, nutrient-rich soil or specialized substrates like ADA Aqua Soil are ideal. For non-planted tanks, you can use gravel, sand, or crushed coral.

How do I cycle my aquarium?

Cycling involves establishing beneficial bacteria that break down harmful ammonia and nitrites. This can take several weeks and involves adding an ammonia source (like fish food or pure ammonia) and monitoring the water parameters. A fishless cycle is the safest method.

How often should I change the water?

Typically, 25-30% of the water should be changed every 1-2 weeks, depending on the tank's stocking level and filtration system. More frequent water changes may be necessary for tanks with higher bio-loads.

What fish can I keep in a planted tank?

Many species of fish can coexist with plants, such as tetras, guppies, bettas, and Corydoras. However, avoid fish that are known to uproot plants, like goldfish or certain cichlids.

How do I choose the right plants for my aquarium?

Choose plants based on your tank's lighting and CO2 availability. Low-light plants like Anubias, Java Fern, and Cryptocoryne are great for beginners. For a high-tech setup, you can include fast-growing plants like Rotala, Glossostigma, and Hemianthus.

Can I keep snails or shrimp in my aquarium?

Yes, snails and shrimp can be beneficial in aquariums. Snails help clean up algae, while shrimp (like Amano shrimp or Cherry shrimp) can help with algae and detritus. Just be mindful of the fish species, as some might eat them.

Why are my plants not growing well?

Plant health can be affected by insufficient light, CO2, nutrients, or poor water conditions. Ensure your plants have the right balance of these factors, and consider dosing liquid fertilizers if needed.

What should my water parameters be for a healthy aquarium?

Ideal parameters can vary depending on the species of fish and plants, but common ranges are:
pH: 6.5–7.5
Ammonia: 0 ppm
Nitrites: 0 ppm
Nitrates: Below 20 ppm for planted tanks (up to 50 ppm for fish-only)
Temperature: 72-78°F (22-26°C) for tropical fish

How can I prevent algae growth?

Algae thrive on excess nutrients and light. To prevent algae, reduce lighting to 8-10 hours a day, avoid overfeeding, maintain proper water changes, and ensure adequate plant growth to compete with algae for nutrients.

How do I test aquarium water?

You can use liquid test kits or test strips to measure water parameters. Key parameters to test are ammonia, nitrites, nitrates, pH, hardness (GH and KH), and temperature.

What is the nitrogen cycle in an aquarium?

The nitrogen cycle refers to the process in which ammonia from fish waste and decomposing food is converted to nitrites and then to nitrates by beneficial bacteria. This cycle is crucial for maintaining a stable and healthy environment for your aquarium's inhabitants.

Why is my aquarium water cloudy?

Cloudy water can result from several factors, including an early stage of the nitrogen cycle, overfeeding, poor filtration, or the presence of algae. Ensure the filter is working properly and reduce feeding.

What should I do if my fish are sick?

Identify the symptoms (e.g., spots, lesions, abnormal behavior) and check water parameters. You may need to quarantine the affected fish and treat them with appropriate medications based on the illness. Regular water changes and maintaining optimal water quality can help prevent disease outbreaks.

How can I prevent fish stress?

Fish stress can be caused by poor water quality, overcrowding, incompatible tank mates, or sudden changes in temperature or lighting. Provide a calm environment, keep water parameters stable, and ensure a proper tank size.
