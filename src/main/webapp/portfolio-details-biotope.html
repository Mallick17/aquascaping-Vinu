Biotope Aquascape Style Overview

**Biotope aquascaping** is the art of recreating natural ecosystems within an aquarium. The goal is to simulate a specific geographic area, such as a river, lake, or rainforest, by using plants, fish, and hardscape materials native to that ecosystem. Biotope aquascapes are highly specialized and strive to create an authentic, balanced representation of a natural environment.

This style emphasizes **authenticity**, so the plants and animals chosen must be native to the selected biotope. Hardscape materials such as **stones, driftwood, and substrate** are also chosen to match the natural environment. Biotope aquascapes can be tailored to match specific regions, such as the **Amazon River**, the **African Rift Lakes**, or a **Southeast Asian stream**.

Key Features:

Specific Geographic Focus:

The aquascape replicates a **specific biome**, such as a tropical river, lake, or wetland. The focus is on using native plants, fish, and other aquatic creatures from that region.

Authenticity and Detail:

Every element in the biotope should be carefully selected to reflect the natural environment as closely as possible. This includes plant species, fish species, and even the type of substrate or rocks used.

Minimalism in Hardscape:

The hardscape should be representative of the biotope, such as rocks, sand, or driftwood, carefully arranged to mimic the natural surroundings. The aim is to avoid unnecessary decoration, focusing on what would be found in nature.

Water Parameters:

Water parameters (temperature, pH, hardness) should be adjusted to replicate the natural conditions of the chosen biotope. This may include acidic, soft water for an Amazon setup or alkaline, hard water for African rift lakes.
